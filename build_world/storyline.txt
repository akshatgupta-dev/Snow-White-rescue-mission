
Cinderella arrives at **SAMK late at night** on a mission to rescue Snow White. The campus is dark and quiet, and everything feels just a little too dramatic for a normal school evening. She knows the first clue must be in the library, but the doors are locked. To get in, she has to prove she belongs there by answering a question: **what does SAMK stand for?** When she gets it right, the library doors slide open as if they had been waiting for her all along.

Inside the library, Cinderella finds a **map of Pori**. Next to it is a note telling her that the weapon she will need is not in the library at all, but in **Agora Hall**. So the library is not the place where she gets stronger — it is the place where she gets direction.

She follows the clue to **Agora Hall**, where things become much stranger. In the middle of the hall, the **Seven Dwarfs** are standing together in matching badminton outfits, guarding a lightsaber as if this is the most reasonable situation in the world. They challenge Cinderella to one badminton round. During the match, all seven dwarfs rush to the front of the court at once, leaving empty space behind them. Cinderella realizes exactly what to do and sends the shuttle behind them. The dwarfs all spin around in panic, bump into each other, and collapse into a complete mess. After a long awkward silence, Doc admits defeat and hands her the **lightsaber**.

With the weapon finally hers, Cinderella heads to the **SAMK cafeteria**. There, sitting all alone, is a beautiful pumpkin. Since pumpkin is her favorite snack, she is about to eat it right away. But just as she lifts it, the clock strikes **10:00 PM**. At that exact moment, the pumpkin transforms into a magical carriage. Cinderella stares at it in complete disbelief and just says, **“…ok. Magic.”** Then the carriage carries her away toward **Kirjurinluoto**.

But the magic does not last forever. On the way to the bridge, the carriage turns back into an ordinary pumpkin. Cinderella, now very hungry, is just about to finally take a bite when she hears someone crying. The sound comes from a **bear** sitting near the bridge. The bear is supposed to be guarding the crossing, but in reality it is simply too hungry to do its job properly. Cinderella looks at the pumpkin, looks at the miserable bear, and gives it away. The bear brightens immediately, eats happily, and then reveals that it is actually the **guardian of Pori and of Kirjurinluoto**. Because Cinderella showed kindness, the bear allows her to pass. So she continues on into Kirjurinluoto — heroically, but still hungry.

In **Kirjurinluoto**, the park is covered in fog and strange magic. The Evil Queen has filled it with false paths and illusions, so Cinderella can only move forward by proving that she truly knows where she is. She solves the local puzzle and finds the correct way through the park.

At the center of Kirjurinluoto, she reaches the **Evil Queen’s stage**. The Queen is waiting there, making the whole thing feel like a dramatic final performance. This is where Cinderella uses the lightsaber she earned in Agora Hall. With it, she defeats the Queen and clears the way to Snow White.

In the final chamber, Snow White is trapped inside magic. Cinderella frees her at last, and the rescue is complete. But immediately after the emotional reunion, both of them hear a loud growling sound. It is their stomachs. They look around and notice a shiny, beautiful apple. The player gets one last choice: eat it or not. If they choose to eat it, the apple restores their health, and they leave feeling stronger. It becomes a small final lesson: **an apple a day keeps the doctor away**.

So the story ends not only with Snow White rescued, but with a simple moral:

**Be kind. Exercise. Go study. And eat healthy.**
